SERUM CREATININE

Rarely do people check their serum creatinine levels even though they are a sure sign your kidneys are failing

Creatinine is a waste product your body releases, mostly by muscle tissues. Your kidneys go to work to filter this waste product as it's accumulation may pause a threat to your Health. To check kidney functionality, the serum creatinine is measured. This is simply how much of this waste product is in your blood. If a moderately high proportion is found, it is safe to say that your kidneys may be having a problem as they are the only ones tasked with the responisbility of filtering it out. However serum creatinine is not something that puffs up with symptoms when it accumulates, hence it is usually detected during routine screening. Because not many people take on routine screening on a regular basis; due to financial burden or for other reasons, this contributes greatly to why kidney failure is not detected in it's early phases. Once symptoms appear, it is quite the sure guarantee that the kidney failure has advancement. The more severe the symptoms, the greater the damage.

AGE

Generally kidney functionality appears to decrease with age.

The earliest appearance of chronic Kidney failure occurs at age range between 5-9, which is quite scary, because nobody is spared. However as people age, the risk of chronic kidney disease increases. There are a few rare exceptions in their 60s who may be spared of this bane.
